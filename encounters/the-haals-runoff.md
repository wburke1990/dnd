# The Haals Runoff — the first eco-terror job

> **A side quest / investigation.** Two neighbouring towns in
> [Suartleheim Eet](../lore/nila-atlas.md) share one lake and one river, yet
> **[Haagen](../lore/nila-atlas.md) is ringed in rich farmland** while
> **[Copaa](../lore/nila-atlas.md) is stuck in poor grassland.** Why? Because the
> **[Company](../lore/the-company.md)'s dwarf-run mine at
> [Haals](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** is dumping its runoff into the watershed and
> **poisoning the downstream fields.** The campaign's **first ecological-atrocity
> job** — investigate it, and decide what to do about the people doing it.
> From the 6/28 brainstorm. Legend: `[settled]` · `[loose]` · `[OPEN]` · 🔒 secret.

---

## 1. The puzzle the party can see `[settled — brainstorm]`

Both towns sit on the **same major lake and river** ([Suartleheim Eet](../lore/nila-atlas.md),
the SE region around Maalm), so neither is short of water — yet their land tells two
different stories:

- **[Haagen](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** (city, north of the lake, **upstream**) is **ringed
  in fertile farmland.**
- **[Copaa](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** (town, south by the lake, **downstream**) sits in
  **scrubby grassland** that won't take a crop, despite all the water it could want.

The tell is the **water itself:** there is plenty of it, but Copaa's is **bad.** A party
that asks the right questions — sick livestock, fish kills, a taste to the well water,
fields that fail no matter the season — is being pointed **upstream.**

> **This does not overwrite the region's natural dryness.** [Maalm](../lore/maalm-the-elven-stronghold.md)
> and its corner are baseline-poor from the **rain-shadow** off Musleheim's western
> mountains (see [the drying of the valley](../lore/the-drying-of-the-valley.md)) — the
> *climate* sets the floor. The Haals runoff is a **second, human-caused blight stacked
> on top of it,** which is exactly why Copaa fails where upstream Haagen, on the clean
> reach, does not. Climate explains the region; the mine explains the *difference between
> the two towns.*

## 2. The cause — the Company's mine at Haals 🔒 `[settled — brainstorm]`

**[Haals](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** is a **mining village** east of Copaa on the lake.
The thing the party will find odd — *why are there dwarves mining here, in the east?* —
is the thread: dwarves' traditional country is **Suartleheim Weet** (the western tin
granite; their yards at **Ools**). The Haals crews are **[Company](../lore/the-company.md)
contractors, not natives** — hired hands stripping the **untapped eastern ore** the
dwarves never worked (see [the Company §3](../lore/the-company.md)).

- **The runoff.** Their tailings and wash — `[OPEN]` heavy-metal, alchemical, or
  faintly magical — go **straight into the lake,** and the **river carries the poison
  down to Copaa's fields.** Haagen, **upstream** on the clean reach, is untouched. One
  watershed, two fates.
- **Who owns it.** `[settled — 6/30 brainstorm]` The
  [East India Company](../lore/the-company.md) is the mine's client, **finances the whole Copaa/Haals/Haagen trade,** and is the **majority shareholder
  in the Haals mines** themselves, shipping through the regional ports at
  **[Aar and Aal](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** (Aar for Copaa/Haals, Aal for Haagen). The
  externality below is the Company's balance sheet made flesh.
- **"Managed," not stopped.** `[loose]` The operation may run cleaner now than it once
  did — enough that the **acute** harm to *people* has faded, leaving a **chronic
  environmental** wound: dead soil, bad water, a grassland that used to be farmland.
  (This is the hook for the **leprosy** link, now canon — see §4.)

## 3. The job — and why it's "eco-terror" `[loose]`

The villain is the **Company** (its mine), so this is the party's **first clean swing at
the colonial-extraction machine** — and, per the DMing principles
([overview §4](../lore/campaign-overview.md)), there is **always a non-combat option**
and **no railroad.** Ways in and ways through:

- **Hooks.** Copaa's farmers or town elders hire the party to find out why their land is
  dying; or the party notices on their own passing through; or a
  **[half-orc skipper](../lore/the-muspel-empire.md)** / a
  [Second Hand](../characters/the-second-hand.md) contact — people the Company is also
  squeezing — points them at Haals.
- **Expose it.** Bring proof to the magistrates — except the **Company owns the
  magistrates** ([the Company §4](../lore/the-company.md)), so the legal road runs into
  the same rot the party meets in Maalm. A lesson in *who the law is for.*
- **Fix it.** Divert the tailings, cap the runoff, clean the reach — an
  engineering/skill puzzle, and the option that doesn't put anyone out of work.
- **Burn it down.** The **eco-terror** path: sabotage or destroy the mine. Satisfying,
  and **morally loaded** — the dwarf miners are **hired hands,** not the villains
  (cf. [Doruk Pell](tavern-name-hunter-crews.md)); wrecking the works hurts the workers,
  not the [Company](../lore/the-company.md) board safe in [Noku](../lore/nila-atlas.md).
  The campaign's *no-clean-answer* thesis ([overview §2–4](../lore/campaign-overview.md))
  in miniature.

## 4. Connections

- **[The Company](../lore/the-company.md)** — this is one of its extraction sites; the
  runoff is the **externality** the campaign uses to make the Company *felt* rather than described. A good **first, local** encounter with the enemy long before the party
  understands the whole shape of it.
- **The dwarves of [Suartleheim](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** — Haals proves the Company's
  reach: dwarf crews working far from home as contractors (the Company §3).
- 🔒 `[settled — 6/30 brainstorm]` **The valley lepers — now canon.** The
  [lepers sheltering in the tombs near Maalm](valley-of-the-kings.md) **are** the
  runoff's human victims: **the leprosy here is a *magical* sickness caused by the
  mine runoff,** and **the lepers are originally from [Copaa](../lore/nila-atlas.md)** —
  driven out of the poisoned downstream town and into the tombs. *(This resolves the
  earlier `[loose]` tie in favor of connecting them; David's timing worry is settled.)*
- **[The drying of the valley](../lore/the-drying-of-the-valley.md)** — the climate
  baseline this blight is layered on (§1).
