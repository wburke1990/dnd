# The Descent of Inanna

## A D&D Quest — Design Note

*The Sumerian [Descent of Inanna](../lore/campaign-overview.md#d-the-weirdness-of-ancestral-myth)
played straight — the myth as literal truth, exact names kept, the weirdness
and the cruelty intact. Set in the **emptied demon deep-city beneath
[Musleheim](../lore/musleheim.md)**: when the hotspot's heat moved south the
demon-people followed it to the living city under the Kurunatukas, and this
city went dark. One queen stayed. The land above her has been dying for a
thousand years, and the party is about to find out why.*

# THE STORY, PLAYED STRAIGHT

The whole quest is one myth followed down as if it were true. In order:

1. **The marriage that greened the world.** In the alliance age — demons and
   mortals still partners, before the binding — **Inanna**, demon-queen who
   rose to rule the surface, married **Dumuzi**, an earth-spirit, the renewer
   whose rising greens the land. Their union guaranteed the world's fertility.
   This is the real *[Courtship of Inanna and Dumuzi](../handouts/)*, the sacred
   marriage, and it belongs on the walls of the older temples (below), verbatim.

2. **The first death — Gugalanna.** Inanna's sister **Ereshkigal** is queen of
   the deep-city. Ereshkigal's husband is **Gugalanna, the Bull of Heaven** — a
   comet-beast (stones fall, become mythic beasts). Inanna's own quarrel gets
   him killed: she sends the Bull against the heroes, and they cut it down.
   Ereshkigal is widowed by her sister's temper.

3. **The descent.** Inanna comes down to the deep-city, giving the funeral of
   Gugalanna as her reason at the gate. Ereshkigal — bereaved, and knowing whose
   fault the funeral is — has the gatekeeper **Neti** bolt the seven gates and
   admit her one at a time, stripping a piece of her power at each. Inanna
   arrives naked and bowed, the judges fix the eye of death on her, and she is
   killed and hung on a hook.

4. **The rescue and the price.** **Enki** makes two sexless beings, the
   **kurgarra** and **galatur**, and sends them down through the cracks — not
   the gates — to revive her. But nothing leaves the underworld free: a life
   must stay in her place. The **galla** follow her up to collect a substitute.

5. **The second death — Dumuzi.** Inanna climbs out, finds Dumuzi on his
   throne *not mourning her*, and out of spite fixes the eye of death on him and
   tells the galla: *take him.* So Inanna is culpable for both husbands — she
   gets Gugalanna killed and she damns her own Dumuzi.

6. **Why it never healed.** In the myth Dumuzi's sister **Geshtinanna** takes
   half his sentence so he can rise half the year — that is the seasons. **Here
   the swap never completed.** The **~1,000-ya Ragnarok pass** hit; the surface
   fell into chaos; no one came back to trade him out. Dumuzi has been below the
   full millennium, so the world above never renews — the blight — and
   Ereshkigal stayed in her emptying city, keeping vigil over her own dead
   Gugalanna while the sister who widowed her is long gone.

# THE TWO ENGINES, BRAIDED

- **Personal rescue.** [Captain Cassia Roan's crew](../characters/the-second-hand.md)
  — the sibling name-crew Preem also runs, whom the party saved last session —
  went down the front way ahead of them and got processed. They hang stripped in
  Ereshkigal's hall. The party descends to pull them out.
- **Ecological restoration.** The blight above is Dumuzi trapped below. Freeing
  him lets the land renew. The party discovers the barrenness and the rescue are
  the same wound.

They descend to save the crew and learn the world is dying for the same reason.

# THE LOCATIONS

Four sites, each doing one job, visitable in any order — the story assembles
from fragments the way the [Valley of the Kings](valley-of-the-kings.md) told
the afterlife across four tombs.

1. **The blighted surface** `[OPEN — NW lean, TBD David]`. The dead zone above
   the city. The symptom, and where the party picks up the Second Hand's trail
   down.

2. **The courtship temple(s) — the "before."** Open-age ruins carrying the
   *Courtship of Inanna and Dumuzi*: the sacred marriage that greened the world.
   The **verbatim love-poetry handouts** live here, as "ancient scrolls." The
   party learns who Dumuzi was and that his union kept the land alive. Green,
   blooming — the opposite of the surface now.

3. **The descent-and-swap shrine — the "how it broke."** A darker site — a
   shrine to Ereshkigal, or the carved mouth of the tunnels — telling the
   descent, the seven-gate stripping, Gugalanna's death, and Inanna damning
   Dumuzi. Primes the gates.

4. **The descent itself — the deep-city.** The climax. See below.

The two story-temples let the players *earn* the tragedy before they reach it,
so freeing Dumuzi lands as fixing something they understand.

# RUNNING THE DESCENT

**The agency fix.** Do not cast the players as Inanna — being stripped through
seven gates with no recourse is the myth's power and a table's poison. They are
the **rescuers** (the kurgarra and galatur). Enki's trick is the answer to "how
do they get in without being stripped": they **slip through the cracks**, not
the gates. The underworld here *is* the Abyss tunnel-network, so a tunnel
back-way in is native geography.

**The gates and the covenant.** The seven gates are this deep-city's stretch
of the dead's road — the same kept-road system as the Valley's twelve hours,
under a different funerary tradition (see
[Demons, the Hotspot, and the Deep Cities](../lore/demons-and-the-hotspot.md)).
Each land buried its dead with its own roll: Maalm's dead carried the twelve
keepers of the hours; the dead of this land carried the seven gates, **Neti**
chief among their keepers. The road's law runs both ways: a traveler who
speaks a keeper's covenant name passes free, and a traveler without one may
still be admitted — by submitting to the gate's toll. **The stripping is the
toll.** Inanna paid it. The Second Hand paid it. The party paid it themselves
in the [dream quest](the-dream-quest.md), in offerings and confessions,
without knowing what it was. And the crack-route works *here* because the
city is emptied — in the living city under the Kurunatukas the cracks are not
unattended, which is why the endgame descent needs the names. This descent is
the rehearsal.

**The seven gates, witnessed.** The stripping already happened — to the Second
Hand. As the party creeps past each gate from the inside, each holds one crew
member's taken thing: Cassia's sword, Halvard's holy symbol, Nuru's focus,
Corvin's cloak, and so on. Seven gates, the crew disassembled across them. The
horror is seen, not suffered, and the rescue has a shape: reclaim a piece per
gate on the way out, and the crew comes back with it.

*(Alternate route, if a player insists on the formal gates: run the stripping as
a costed choice — each gate takes a different kind of thing, the player picks
which, and anyone who argues or attacks a gate only takes a penalty. The refrain
holds: "the ways of the underworld are perfect; they may not be questioned."
Force is the losing move, at the gates and at the throne.)*

**Ereshkigal is not a fight.** She is a bereaved queen keeping vigil over
Gugalanna. She cannot be beaten by force — the whole descent teaches that. She
is reached the way the kurgarra and galatur reach her in the myth: by **meeting
her grief instead of her throne** — echoing her pain rather than answering it.
The party that has given up enough of its reflex to power can see the widow
instead of the monster. That is the climax.

**Freeing Dumuzi — no name can do it.** Dumuzi is of the **nameless earth
lineage** (see [lineages & afterlives](../lore/lineages-and-afterlives.md)):
there is no true name to find, so the campaign's central tool gets no grip
here. Let the players feel that — a party a dozen tombs into a name-hunt will
reach for a name, and the walls of the courtship temples hold everything about
him except one. The nameless cannot be owned, and cannot be ransomed by
ownership either. He is held by the underworld's law, not by a leash — a life
for a life — and the only door out is the **substitution** (below). This is
also why the blight has stood for a thousand years: there was never a name to
speak, so no wizard could shortcut it. Mercy here cannot be cast. It has to be
paid. The land heals as he rises.

**The substitute price.** A life for a life is the underworld's law. Lifting the
crew, or Dumuzi, or both, calls for a substitute the galla will accept. The
intended resolution is **Geshtinanna** — Dumuzi's sister, come at last to take
the half-sentence she was always meant to, a thousand years late. `[OPEN]`
whether the party can find another answer, or whether the choice falls on them.

**Neti's name.** The gatekeeper's covenant name is written in the burials near
the descent-and-swap shrine — this land's dead were interred with their
gatekeepers' names as safe-conduct, the same way Maalm's dead carried the
twelve hours — and the party can **send it up to Preem or keep it.**
Chain the doorkeeper who was only doing his job, or don't. The name quest's
whole moral argument, in one small, low-stakes rehearsal.

# THE PAX ECHO

[Pax Verdant](../characters/pax.md) is **not** Dumuzi's literal heir — his
lineage is a blank, and the design keeps his "chosen vs. deluded" question open
until his death. So play it as an **echo**: Pax, the green-touched one who
believes the living world chose him, feels the trapped earth-spirit calling.
It might be true kinship. It might be his own certainty pattern-matching a wound
he was always going to find. The quest is one more test that refuses to resolve
him — which is exactly what his arc wants.
