# Tavern Scene — Rival Name-Hunter Crews

**Scene trigger:** the party returns to Maalm after **tomb four** and heads to
the tavern. Two of Preem's other name-hunter crews are drinking there. They are
**not** a contrivance: Preem deliberately runs multiple crews against the ~30
scattered sites on the map he hands out — a race for the names. Maalm is the
staging point and the place crews report back to Preem and resupply.

By now the **party are the seasoned ones** — four tombs deep. The two crews
they meet here are **newer**: just commissioned, or a tomb or two in at most.
So the PCs walk in *ahead* of these rivals, which flips the usual dynamic — the
party are the grizzled outfit the newbies size up. The tension is rivalry plus
the friction between the two crews themselves.

> **Geography (confirm):** the **Valley of the Kings** (see
> [`valley-of-the-kings.md`](valley-of-the-kings.md)) is the one site within
> walking distance of Maalm, and it holds the numbered tombs the party is
> working through — tombs one, two, three, and now four. That's why the party
> loops back to Maalm between runs: the Valley is right there. The ~29
> far-flung sites are the wider map — for later, or for other crews. No crew
> working those distant sites would backtrack to the city, which is exactly why
> the haunted crew (below) belongs in a *later* town, not here.

All crews use standard species (halflings, half-orcs, elves, dwarves, humans,
gnomes), mixed like Maalm's lower city. Names kept short and consonant-heavy
to match the Maalm/Egyptian flavor.

---

## Group 1 — The Lapis Writ

*Chartered, funded, smug. They flash Preem's seal like a badge and treat the
job as a prestigious commission, not grave-robbing. They think the names are
just loot.*

| # | Name | Species | Role | Hook |
|---|------|---------|------|------|
| 1 | **Setha Renk** | Half-elf | Leader / face | Founder-blood on her mother's side and won't let you forget it; treats the commission as an honor |
| 2 | **Doruk Pell** | Dwarf | Vault-breaker | Tomb engineer — traps, seals, load-bearing math; the only one who respects the dead |
| 3 | **Issa Quib** | Halfling | Scribe / namer | Copies true names into a ledger with gloves on; uneasy in a way she can't name yet |
| 4 | **Gharn** | Half-orc | Muscle | Bored, loyal, cracks jokes; the heart of the group and the one the party might actually like |
| 5 | **Vael Astre** | Elf | Arcanist | Preem's golden boy; openly competitive with the PCs, hints he knows what the names are *for* |

**Tavern angle:** Setha buys a round to look magnanimous, then needles the
party. Vael watches the PCs too closely. Gharn defects from the smugness and
talks straight if you get him alone.

## Group 2 — The Brass Jackals

*Foreign freelancers from the north (Kuru), here for the gold and nothing
else. No reverence for Maalm, no patience for elven pedigree. Loud.*

| # | Name | Species | Role | Hook |
|---|------|---------|------|------|
| 1 | **Veska Tull** | Human | Leader | Ex-Kuru caravan-master; treats the job as a contract, not a calling — would happily cut a side deal with the PCs |
| 2 | **Mogg** | Half-orc | Enforcer | Big, deadpan, funnier than he has any right to be |
| 3 | **Pim Riddle** | Gnome | Trapsmith / alchemist | Solves every problem with more blasting powder than it needs |
| 4 | **Hadda Vimm** | Dwarf | Quartermaster | The only one actually reading the map; quietly runs the crew |
| 5 | **Sefi** | Halfling | Scout | Youngest, light-fingered, in over her head and hiding it |

**Tavern angle:** The Lapis Writ believe in the *prestige* of Preem's
commission; the Brass Jackals believe in the payout and sneer at founder-blood
airs. In one taproom they're a half-step from a brawl — the PCs can watch it,
stoke it, or get pulled in. Veska may pitch the party a temporary "screw the
aristocrats" alliance.

---

## DM levers

- **Rivalry:** both crews race the PCs from the same starting line. Let Vael
  (Lapis Writ) and Veska (Jackals) become recurring faces the party roots for
  or against.
- **Inter-crew friction:** the two crews dislike each other on sight. Good for
  a comic/tense set piece that needs no PC input to kick off.
- **Foreshadowing — restrained here.** Issa's unease and Vael's hints are the
  only cracks. The heavy dread is held for the haunted crew below, in a later
  city. Don't over-tip the names plot in Maalm.

# Hold for a later tavern (next city)

## The Ashfoot — the haunted crew

*Do NOT use in Maalm.* This is a crew that has already run the **Valley of the
Kings** and pushed on to the scattered sites — so they only make sense
encountered **far from Maalm**, in a later city, re-provisioning between
distant tombs. That distance is what sells "they've been out in it."

*Ragged, lean, been at it a season too long. They've started to suspect what
they're really carrying out — without the vocabulary for it yet.*

| # | Name | Species | Role | Hook |
|---|------|---------|------|------|
| 1 | **Korbo Sann** | Half-orc | Leader | Tired, watchful; has buried crew and counts heads constantly |
| 2 | **Nem the Pale** | Human | Tomb-guide | Knows the tombs better than anyone; flinches at the word "names" |
| 3 | **Bittel Roon** | Halfling | Thief | Twitchy; pocketing names to sell on the side — a loose thread that could blow Preem's scheme open |
| 4 | **Hessa Drum** | Dwarf | Healer / lapsed priest | Quietly convinced the crews are doing something damnable; drinks to not think about it |
| 5 | **Olen Vask** | Elf | Survivor | Sole holdover from an *earlier* crew that didn't come back; says little, knows the most |

**Valley of the Kings backstory (concrete foreshadowing):** They cleared a
tomb in the Valley — the same run the PCs make at the campaign's start. They
came back with names and a crewmate short. **Olen** wasn't originally theirs:
Korbo's people found him alone and half-unstrung in the tombs, the last of an
earlier Preem crew that never reported back, and folded him in — which is why
he's done the Valley twice and says the least. The thing that rattled the rest
is smaller and worse, and it has to be supernatural — the tombs are 5,000 years
old; nothing *living* is still down there to answer to anything. The only thing
that survives with a true name is the **sentinel** left to guard the tomb: a
bound spirit, a desiccated watchman, a lesser demon sealed at the threshold —
a thing that had kept its own will for five thousand years. **Nem** read a name
off the wall, or **Bittel** lifted the tablet it was carved on, and the
guardian *answered to that name* — knelt, went slack, and stopped being itself.
A will older than Maalm, erased by a spoken word. They don't understand it.
They just know names **do something** — they can own a thing that should have
been unownable — and they want to be far away before they have to think about
it.

**Why it lands later:** by the time the party meets the Ashfoot, the PCs have
done the Valley themselves and carry their own names. The Ashfoot are the
party's near future walking back out the door — the seed of *names own people,
the servants aren't servants* — delivered by a crew that lived it without
understanding it. Olen gives the first real warning; Hessa confirms it from
the bottom of a cup. No exposition required.
