# Session 03 — Tombs II & III: The Lions and the Mummy
## The Valley of the Kings · two more tombs cleared

---

# RECAP

Forewarned by the Set-beast (the forked-tail jackal they spoke with on the
road in Session 2), the party pressed deeper into the **Valley of the
Kings** and worked through **Tomb II** and **Tomb III** — the session's two
big set pieces were the **cave lions** and the **mummy**.

---

# WHAT HAPPENED

## The lions (Tomb II)

The party reached the **cave lions** — the servants of the old gods that
keep Tomb II — already primed by the Set-beast's warning to handle them
carefully rather than charge in.

- They **fed the lions a lot of rat meat.** Where the rat-meat gambit had
  fallen flat or been merely tolerated before, here it *worked*: the
  offering bought them passage / goodwill instead of a fight with a pride
  that uses pack tactics and doesn't pursue past the entrance chamber.
- The party got through Tomb II without the lions turning on them.

> **Rat-meat running gag:** three
> sessions of offering rat meat to everything, and it's the *lions* (set up
> by the Set-beast's tip in Session 2) where it works. Lean into that.

## The mummy — Kethara (Tomb III / the Valley dead)

The party had their **mummy encounter** with **Kethara** (Farseh-ti) — the
ancient grief-made-undead wife of Amseh IV, who has kept her vigil in the
Valley for thousands of years and watches over the lepers sheltering among
the tombs. She is not a monster to be put down; she's sorrow with limited
healing powers and a curse for anyone who harms those under her protection.

- The party came through the encounter and **cleared Tombs II and III**,
  recovering what each held (the **names**, per the commission).

---

# DM NOTES & OPEN THREADS

- **Rat meat: resolved (gloriously).** The lions accepted it. If the
  party keeps the bit going, that's fine — but the gag has had its big
  moment.
- **Kethara / the lepers.** Note for continuity how the party treated the
  lepers and Kethara — whether they were respectful, whether they triggered
  (or avoided) her Charisma curse, and whether they connected her to the
  Valley's deeper history. This shapes whether the Valley stays a safe
  waypoint or turns hostile.
- **Tomb IV remains.** Three of the four Amseh tombs are now cleared
  (I in Session 2; II and III here). **Tomb IV — King Amseh IV, Kethara's
  husband — is still sealed**, and it's the emotional/thematic capstone of
  the Valley. Good place to resume next session.
- **The names ledger.** The party has now pulled names from three tombs and
  is carrying them back toward Preem. This is the campaign's slow-burn hook:
  they're learning, without knowing it, what a true name can do to a soul.
