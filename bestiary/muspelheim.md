# Musleheim — Hunting & Foraging Table (Irish Myth)

20 hunts in Muspelheim that could go wrong or right, based on Irish myths. The
Norse fire-island runs on a Celtic-weird bestiary: most prey is safe, but the
mythic beasts carry taboos — boons for the right interaction, curses for a careless
kill or a hungry mouth. (Musleheim's region notes: [musleheim.md](../lore/musleheim.md).)

---

## The Myths

**The Salmon of Knowledge (Fintan / Finnegas & Fionn mac Cumhaill)**
Whoever eats this salmon, caught in the Boyne, gains all wisdom in the world. Fionn accidentally gains this power when cooking it. (+2 Int)

**The Enbarr of Manannán (Magical Horse)**
Manannán mac Lir's horse could gallop over sea and land. Mortal riders risk being swept into the Otherworld forever.

**The Hound of Culann (Cú Chulainn's Naming Story)**
Young Setanta kills Culann's guard dog and takes its place until a replacement can be raised, becoming "the Hound of Culann." (Curse for killing the hound, curse reversed and learn battle maneuver for agreeing to protect the house)

**The Children of Lir (Swan Transformation)**
Lir's children are cursed by their jealous stepmother to live as swans for 900 years. Swan-hunting would be a grave taboo. (Healing Magic + Temp HP if they don't kill the swans, curse if they do)

**Oisín and Niamh's White Horse**
Niamh brings Oisín to Tír na nÓg on a white horse; stepping off it years later, he instantly ages to dust. (No effect)

**The Boar of Benbulben (Death of Diarmuid)**
Diarmuid Ua Duibhne is fatally wounded by a magical boar he is fated never to hunt safely, despite being a great warrior. (Hard fight with a giant Boar)

**The Hounds of Fionn mac Cumhaill (Bran and Sceólang)**
Born from a woman transformed into a hound, Fionn's dogs are supernatural, wise, and loyal—never to be killed or eaten. (Curse if they kill them, discover shapeshifter + receive boon if they follow them and fight off the other hounds)

**The Salmon of Assaroe**
Another magical salmon said to leap a waterfall backwards, guarding Otherworldly wisdom. (+2 WIS)

**The Black Pig of Mucklagh**
A monstrous pig that rampages through Offaly, plowing furrows in the land—hunting it is perilous. (Hard fight with giant pig)

**The Fox of Knockgraffon**
A fairy tale variant—foxes appear as messengers of the Otherworld; harming them often brings curses. (Cool info if they dont kill them, curse if they do)

**The Deer of Sídhe**
Many heroes, including Oisín, chase white stags that lead them into the Otherworld—often these deer are shape-shifted women. (Transform back into women + curse if they kill them, benefit??)

**Cormac and the Fairy Boar**
King Cormac mac Airt hunts a boar that leads him to a magical Otherworld feast hall.

**The Crane-Bag of Manannán**
Made from the skin of a magical crane, it held treasures of the sea-god. Cranes themselves are liminal animals in Irish lore.

**The King's Sons and the Grey of Macha (Magical Horses)**
Horses with prophetic powers or supernatural speed often show up—sometimes they refuse to carry certain riders.

**The Phantom Hound of Connacht**
A spectral hound that guards treasure—harmless if respected, deadly if attacked. https://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:mordenkainens-faithful-hound

**The Cow of Plenty (Glas Gaibhnenn)**
A magical cow that could feed entire armies with endless milk. Attempting to steal or kill it leads to disaster.

**The Enchanted Boar of Mac Dá Thó**
A huge boar is served at a feast, sparking conflict between Ulstermen and Connachtmen—boars symbolize strife.

**The White Birds of Aonghus Óg**
The god of love transforms lovers into swans; harming swans near his sídhe is sacrilege.

**The Hound of Ailill mac Máta**
The king of Connacht's hound was said to know friend from foe; killing it would draw the wrath of the province.

**The Bees of Myth**
Bees were considered messengers from the Otherworld; their honey was sacred. To eat cursed honey meant binding yourself to fairy power. (Curse for eating the honey, information for listening to the bees)

## 🍀 Muspelheim Hunting Quick Reference

When the party hunts or forages:

- Roll 1d20 on the table.
- **1–7 = Mundane prey (safe).**
- **8–20 = Mythic prey (dangerous if eaten).**
- PCs may roll Arcana / Nature / History (DC 14) to recognize the mythic animal.
  - Success: they know the taboo.
  - Failure: they think it's safe food.

| d20 | Creature / Legend | Encounter Hook | If Eaten… (Mechanical Effect) |
|----:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Salmon of Knowledge | PCs catch a shining salmon in a sacred river | Gain +2 permanent INT, but must save vs WIS (DC 15) or be haunted by overwhelming visions (disadv. on Perception checks until a long rest). |
| 2 | Enbarr, Horse of Manannán | A white horse approaches camp, neighing softly | Eating its flesh binds your soul to the sea-god: teleport to the ocean (DM's choice where). |
| 3 | Culann's Hound | A great hound blocks a passage, guarding a hall | Eating it transforms a PC into a dire hound for 1d4 days, losing speech but gaining scent-tracking. |
| 4 | Children of Lir (Swans) | Four swans sing mournful songs nearby | Eating swan-flesh gives cursed voice: speech becomes painful song, disadv. on Persuasion until curse lifted. |
| 5 | Niamh's White Horse | A white steed grazes in a clearing | Eating it causes rapid aging: 1 year per bite. Only divine intervention reverses it. |
| 6 | Boar of Benbulben | PCs hear it tearing through the underbrush | Eating it inflicts boar's bane: max HP reduced by 1d6 permanently. |
| 7 | Bran & Sceólang (Fionn's Hounds) | Twin hounds circle camp, watching | Eating them curses you with eternal hunger (must eat 2× daily rations or take exhaustion). |
| 8 | Salmon of Assaroe | A salmon leaps a waterfall backwards | Eating it grants truesight 30 ft for 24 hours, but you are compelled to seek the Otherworld. |
