# The Maalm Watch — Corrupt City Guards

> **Enemy mook block.** The bought city watch that does
> [Ratatat](the-rat-man.md)'s muscle work — the same rot from
> [the square](../encounters/the-second-hand-ambush.md) that pins the Second
> Hand, and the force that moves to "arrest" the party at
> [the docks](../encounters/docks-departure.md). **One stat block covers every
> guard;** scale the fight by count and waves, not by fiddling each body.
> Cassia Roan wore this badge before her sergeant **Marcellus** ran her out for
> naming the racket (see [the Second Hand](../characters/the-second-hand.md)).
>
> Built for **5 PCs at level 2–3, D&D 2024 rules,** fighting **with** the four
> Second Hand allies. See the [docks encounter](../encounters/docks-departure.md)
> for wave counts and the level-2 vs level-3 dials.
>

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 14 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 13 (+1) | 10 (+0) | 11 (+0) | 10 (+0) |

- **Skills** Perception +2, Intimidation +2
- **Senses** Passive Perception 12

**Pack Tactics.** A watchman has **advantage** on an attack roll against a
creature if at least one of the watchman's allies is within 5 ft. of it and not
incapacitated. *(This is why numbers hurt — a knot of guards on one PC hits
hard even though each guard is weak.)*

### Actions

- **Multiattack.** The watchman makes one attack, or two while at least half the
  starting wave is still standing (they're bold in a crowd, tentative once
  thinned).
- **Spear.** *Melee or Thrown Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range
  20/60 ft. *Hit:* 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing.
- **Light Crossbow.** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, range 80/320 ft.
  *Hit:* 5 (1d8 + 1) piercing.
- **Clap in Irons.** In place of an attack, a watchman adjacent to a **grappled,
  prone, or restrained** creature can lock manacles on it. Escaping the irons
  takes an action and a **DC 13 Strength (Athletics)** check. *(The "arrest" is
  the pretext; this is how they sell it before the swords come out.)*

## Running the guards

- **They come in waves.** The watch holds the harbor; bodies keep arriving from
  the streets behind the dock. The point isn't a fixed count — it's the sense
  that they don't run out. See the [docks encounter](../encounters/docks-departure.md)
  for the wave schedule and the win condition (casting off, not a body count).
- **They fight for the arrest, not to the death.** Individual watchmen are
  bought, not fanatics. Once a wave drops below half, the survivors hang back,
  throw spears, and wait for the next wave rather than charging a cornered
  party — which is the window the party uses to reach the boat.
- **Pack Tactics rewards spreading them out.** A guard alone is a poor threat; a
  guard with two friends on the same target is dangerous. Let the party feel
  that peeling guards off one another (a choke on the pier, a shove into the
  water, Nuru's control spell) turns the fight.
- **Water is on the table.** The dock, the pier edge, and the gap to the boat
  are all shove range. A watchman in the harbor in chain shirt and shield is out
  of the fight for the round it takes to climb out — a clean, non-lethal way to
  clear bodies.
