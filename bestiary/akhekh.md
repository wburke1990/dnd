# The Akhekh

Full write-up for entry **d20:20** on the [Egypt encounter table](egypt.md) —
the winged serpent-griffin of the deep desert. Compact row lives there; this
file carries the complete stat block, the parley material (the party keeps
*Speak with Animals* up — ruled to work on it: it has no language and an
animal's mind), and the real-world mythology with sources.

*Tags:* egypt-quest; egypt-mythological; d20:20; parley-first; Set; lair often
trapped; cloak materials

## Stat Block

### Akhekh
*Monstrosity (Large, winged serpent-griffin)* — **Monstrosity, CR 7 (2,900 XP)** · Chaotic Neutral
**AC** 16 (natural armor) · **HP** 105
**STR** 19 **DEX** 16 **CON** 17 **INT** 7 **WIS** 12 **CHA** 10
**Speed** 30 ft, fly 60 ft
**Damage Resistances** poison
**Senses** darkvision 60 ft, passive Perception 11
**Languages** none (understands tone, threat, and tribute)

**Sandstorm Aura.** The Akhekh's wingbeats drive a standing veil of sand in a
15-ft cone in the direction it faces. Ranged attacks into or out of the cone
are made at disadvantage.

**Actions**

**Multiattack.** The Akhekh makes two attacks: one with its beak and one with
its tail sting.

**Beak.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft, one target.
*Hit:* 13 (2d8+4) piercing damage.

**Tail Sting.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 10 ft, one target.
*Hit:* 8 (1d8+4) piercing damage plus 10 (3d6) poison damage; DC 15 CON save
for half the poison.

*(Detection, per the table's rules: passive Stealth 13, passive Perception 11.
A level-2 party that fights it straight will almost certainly die — CR 7 is
nearly 3× their deadly budget. The encounter is built to be talked through,
fled, or survived, not won.)*

## What It Is (in Nila)

A chaos-beast of the red land — the deep desert west of the river, Set's
country. It is **fair game: NO CURSE** for killing it (if you somehow can),
and its feathers, woven into a cloak, grant **fire resistance for 1d4 days per
feather** (the BOON — and note it *molts*; a good parley can earn feathers
without a corpse).

But look closer and it wears **three uraei** — royal cobras, pharaonic
regalia — around its crest. In the old empire, akhekhs were **broken to the
chariot**: chaos-power harnessed by kings to dominate other chaos. This one
remembers that, the way an animal remembers — not as history but as a scar.

**And it is being used again.** Set's priests have ringed its lair with traps
and treat the beast as their early-warning system: when the Akhekh screams,
they know someone is coming. It gets nothing from this arrangement. It is,
in the campaign's terms, a *named thing owned by someone else* — the whole
thesis at animal scale.

## Motivation & Parley (Speak with Animals)

**Its one want:** to be *feared on its own ground* — acknowledged, not used.
Terror is tribute; strength is currency. It despises the tame river valley
and everything that smells of it.

**Its voice:** wind-hiss and screech shaped into meaning. Short. Concrete.
It speaks in challenges and appraisals ("*Small. Loud. Which of you is the
strong one?*"), never in sentences longer than a breath.

Run it on the table's **2d6 reaction roll**, with these Akhekh-specific
levers:

- **What raises the mood:** standing ground without attacking (it respects
  nerve — Intimidation and Persuasion work equally well here, and groveling
  actively fails); offering meat; naming it fearsome; backing out of its
  territory slowly, facing it.
- **What lowers it:** drawn bows (its aura exists to spite archers), turning
  your back, smelling of the river valley's temples — and above all anything
  that reads as *harness*: ropes, nets, commands.
- **The lever that opens it up:** the priests. It knows the two-legged ones
  in dark robes ring its home with metal teeth and come running when it
  cries. It lacks the words for "exploitation," but *"they listen to my voice
  and I get nothing"* is exactly within its grasp. A party that offers to
  **break the traps** — or just points out plainly that the beast is a
  watchdog on someone else's leash — can flip it from Wary to Favorable in
  one exchange.

**What a good parley earns:** safe passage; 1d3 molted feathers (the cloak
boon); or — best case — a guide's escort across its stretch of desert, the
Akhekh riding thermals above the party, which incidentally makes every other
wandering-encounter predator keep its distance. **What it will never do:**
be ridden, carry, fetch, or serve. It has done that. Ask, and the
conversation is over.

**If it fights:** it opens with a diving multiattack on whoever holds a bow,
then disengages upward — it kites, it doesn't slug. It breaks off entirely
once bloodied (~52 HP) or once it has downed one creature and been shown real
teeth; it is territorial, not suicidal. A fleeing party is *escorted out*, not
pursued to the death.

**The hook underneath:** its lair sits inside the priests' trap-ring. Any
approach — invited or not — is a trap encounter first, and springing one
tells Set's priests exactly where the party is.

## Real-World Mythology (with sources)

**Thinly attested** in actual Egyptology — essentially two conflicting
19th-century scholarly descriptions, a few ancient depictions, and a lot of
Victorian and internet extrapolation on top. No ancient narrative about it
survives. What's defensible:

- **Appearance — the sources genuinely disagree.** E. A. Wallis Budge (*Gods
  of the Egyptians*, vol. II) describes an **antelope/oryx body with two wings
  and a bird's head crowned with three uraei**; some retellings add a
  serpent's tail. Paul Pierret (*Dictionnaire d'archéologie égyptienne*,
  1875) instead describes an **eagle-headed winged lion — a griffin**. Common
  denominator: a winged desert quadruped, griffin-like. See
  [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akhekh) and
  [A Book of Creatures](https://abookofcreatures.com/2021/02/01/akhekh/)
  (the best-cited secondary source; lays out both with citations). Our version
  splits the difference and keeps the serpent.
- **Set, the desert, chaos.** Consistently an ally or incarnation of **Set**,
  tied to darkness, the western desert, and chaos; the name relates to an
  Egyptian word for **darkness/twilight**. Paul Carus (*History of the
  Devil*, 1900) lists "the griffin (akhekh)" among Set-Typhon's symbols —
  [verified on Wikisource](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_History_of_the_Devil/Chapter_3).
- **Royal-power ambivalence — the best hook, and the one this write-up is
  built on.** Not purely evil: the three uraei are pharaonic regalia,
  **Ramesses II was praised as "an akhekh"** terrorizing the Hittites, and
  the **Metternich Stela** shows an akhekh **drawing a king's chariot over
  two crocodiles** — chaos-power harnessed to dominate other chaos.
- **Modern embellishment, use knowingly:** the "primordial darkness-dragon
  that swallows the sun, oldest Typhonian monster, ancestor of the
  cockatrice" strand traces to **Gerald Massey (1880s)** — a poet/amateur
  mythographer, not accepted Egyptology
  ([Dragon Dreaming's writeup](https://dragondreaming.wordpress.com/2019/03/24/akhekh/)
  traces this). Wiki personalities and hoards are invented.
