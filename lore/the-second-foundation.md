# The Second Foundation

The elven cabal seated in the citadel of Maalm, preparing for the next turn of
the world's cyclical cataclysm — **Ragnarok** — and the chair from which
[Preem](../characters/preem.md) runs his private heresy. Distilled from the
6/8–6/17/26 Discord brainstorm.

> Legend: **[settled]** = agreed in chat · **[loose]** = floated, not locked.

## 1. What It Is

The **Second Foundation** is the inner faction of Maalm's old elven elite. Its
members hold that the world ends and remakes itself on a long cycle — the
cataclysm the campaign calls **Ragnarok** — and that the next one is due
**within roughly a year.** They exist to **prepare** for it. **[settled]**

- They are seated in the **citadel** of Maalm, beside the **council**. Their
  proximity to the seat of power is the whole point of the name: they are a
  *foundation* under the city's authority — and, for the Asimov-literate, a
  knowing nod to the *Second Foundation* of Foundation. **[settled]** (The
  table is not expected to catch the reference — *"I doubt they will figure out
  Mul is The Mule"* — so keep it subtle.)
- **Preem is the First Chair.** His **tower** — the square building on the
  Maalm map, in the citadel space — is his residence and his base of
  operations. **[settled]**
- The Foundation is **not** all of Maalm's power. The **council** of Maalm has
  an **independent faction / leadership outside the Foundation**; not every
  elven elite is a Foundationer, and not every Foundationer answers to Preem.
  He is *close* to power, not in sole command of it. **[settled]**

## 2. Ragnarok — the world's cataclysm cycle

The cosmological frame the Foundation is built around. **[settled in concept;
the tie to the ancient-empire fall synthesizes existing campaign lore.]**

- The world undergoes a recurring, world-breaking cataclysm. The Foundation
  believes the **next one is about a year out** and prepares accordingly.
- **The last Ragnarok already happened in the campaign's deep history.** It is
  the **climate catastrophe — drought, famine, volcanic winter** — that buried
  the **ancient Egyptian-inspired empire** beneath what is now Maalm. (See
  [Maalm: The Founding Story](maalm-founding.md) and
  [The Valley of the Kings](../encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md).)
- The tombs **record that Ragnarok as it was lived**: the **bleeding star**,
  the **seven great thunders**, the rafts of skeletons and the Sea Peoples in
  Amseh III's tomb; the starving land and the sacrificed children in Amseh
  IV's. That was the sky burning the *last* time the world turned over.
- A physical fingerprint ties it together: the **ancient map** carved on the
  valley floor and sealed in Tomb 1 shows a coastline **without three islands
  that exist today** — *"three islands that exist today are absent, the
  northern sea is larger."* Those missing islands are **volcanic**. The last
  Ragnarok's **volcanic winter** is also what **raised the volcanic islands**.
  Fire and ash remade the map. That is what Ragnarok *does*.

This is why a **volcano-demon** is the master key to Preem's endgame: volcanism
is the engine of the cataclysm. Set off the volcanoes and you have set off
Ragnarok — on demand, without waiting for the cycle. See **§4**.

## 3. The Founding link — who the Foundationers are

The Second Foundation grew out of the **elves who settled Maalm after the
demise of the valley kings.** **[settled]**

- The ancient valley kings (the **Amseh** dynasty) died with their empire in
  the last Ragnarok. A thousand years later the **Dragonborn Beowulf** and his
  twelve companions — **elves and men** — arrived and founded Maalm over the
  ruins (see [Maalm: The Founding Story](maalm-founding.md)).
- The **elves among those founders and the old families who followed** became
  Maalm's elite — the deep-rooted old guard of the gated upper city (see
  [Maalm — the elven stronghold](maalm-the-elven-stronghold.md)). The **Second
  Foundation is their inner, forward-looking cabal**: the elves old enough and
  connected enough to be planning for the *next* turn of the cycle, not just
  remembering the last.

## 4. Preem's heresy within the Foundation

The Foundation prepares to **survive** Ragnarok. Preem means to **own** it.
**[settled]** Full character treatment in [Preem](../characters/preem.md);
in brief:

- He uses his prestigious tower-residence to **hide his demon-binding** behind
  the council's authority. **Every servant in the tower is a disguised demon**
  (see [Preem's demons](../characters/preems-demons.md)).
- He has a **post-Ragnarok scheme** to unleash demons once the cataclysm hits —
  a follow-up the rest of the Foundation is not party to.
- And the escalation that makes him dangerous *now*: **why wait a year for the
  next Ragnarok when a bound demon can trigger it whenever he wants?** *"Why
  wait 1000 years for the next Ragnarok when you can just bring it about anytime
  you want with a demon."* A demon **with the power to set off volcanoes** is
  the trigger. *"He wouldn't mind enslaving one last demon… especially if that
  demon has the power to set off volcanoes."*

This is the campaign's long fuse: the patron who hired the party for a tomb
errand is quietly assembling the means to end the world on his own schedule.

## 5. The Plan for Revealing This to the Players

- **Layer one — the patron.** The party knows Preem only as the wizard who pays
  for names and jewels. Nothing overt.
- **Layer two — the question.** A curious PC notices a **wizard seated in a
  place of power** in the citadel and asks *why.* *"Hopefully someone draws that
  link and questions why a wizard is seated in a place of power."* That question
  surfaces the **Second Foundation.**
- **Layer three — the names.** They realize the names they've been selling are
  **fuel for demon-binding**, not antiquarian trivia.
- **Layer four — the demons.** They prove a "person" in the tower is a demon
  (see [Preem's demons](../characters/preems-demons.md)).
- **Layer five — Ragnarok.** They understand the Foundation's premise, and then
  the worse truth: Preem doesn't intend to wait for the cataclysm. He intends to
  **cause** it.
