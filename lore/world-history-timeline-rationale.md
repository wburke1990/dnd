# Why the Timeline Is Dated the Way It Is

> Companion to [the world-history timeline](world-history-timeline.md) — a short note on the
> *reasoning* behind the dates, for review. It all rests on **two fixed pillars** and a handful
> of **physical rules**; everything else is reckoned from those.

## The two pillars

1. **The comet clock (~1,000 years).** A comet returns on a ~1,000-year cycle and every return
   remakes the world. That cadence is the metronome — passes fall at ~**6,000 / 5,000 / 4,000 /
   3,000 / 2,000 / 1,000** ya, and the next is ~now. **Every major turn lands on a pass.**
2. **The Bleeding Star = ~5,000 ya — the one firm anchor.** The Krakatoa-catastrophe is fixed at
   ~5,000 (the tombs and the sixty-foot statues both date it there). It is **Amseh III's Year
   100,** so the whole **Amseh dynasty** is reckoned out from it along the kings' reign-lengths
   (250 + 200 + 120 + 20 yrs → ~5,550–4,960 ya). Every other date hangs off this one.

## The physical rules that fix the rest

- **A comet must actually *arrive* to build land.** So **Musleheim does not exist until the
  ~4,000 pass** raises it — which is why nothing happens *on* it before then, and why Maalm's
  founding (~4,100) is the empty trough *before* the land returns.
- **A bad pass's alchemical sickness leaves land dead for 1,000+ years** (Musleheim is *still*
  half-poisoned a millennium on). So **Lonka** (hit ~5,000) is unlivable until ~3,200, and
  **Kalikhat's surface** until ~2,000 — which is when each can next be settled. (Lonka is resettled
promptly, ~3,000, and held only ~a century before the dragonborn take it back.)
- **The hot spot drifts southeast,** cooling what it leaves behind. The under-sea tunnels off
  **northwest Musleheim** only cool enough to walk **~3,000 ya** — the earliest the dragonborn
  could walk home to Jotunheim.
- **Colonies and armies need *organization,* not just ships.** The dragonborn could always
  *voyage*; large colonial/military moves therefore cluster in the **iron-age empire** era
  (~4,000 on), not the empty Dark Age.

## Event by event (the short why)

| Event | When (ya) | Why then |
|---|---|---|
| Creation — Taniyn & the Phoenix fall | ~6,000 | One comet cycle before the Bleeding Star; the first drop. |
| Golden Lonka's golden age | 6,000–5,000 | The first full cycle, before the Bleeding Star ends it. |
| Amseh dynasty | 5,550–4,960 | Reckoned from the Bleeding Star (Amseh III's Yr 100) along the reign-lengths. |
| **Bleeding Star (Ragnarok I)** | **~5,000** | **The anchor** — comet pass 2; ends Amseh, shatters Lonka, sickens Kalikhat, collapses the Bronze Age. |
| The Dark Age | 4,960–4,100 | From the Amseh collapse to Maalm's founding — the empty trough between passes. |
| Maalm founded (Bedraageon) | ~4,100 | Set *before* the ~4,000 pass returns land, so Bedraageon finds an empty world and no other dragonborn. |
| Musleheim raised; Muspel empire & Iron Age begin | ~4,000 | The comet pass that builds the fire-island; nothing on Musleheim can predate it. |
| Lonka habitable → Jotunheim resettled by Maalm colonists (elves and men) | ~3,200 → ~3,000 | The ~5,000 sickness finally fades, after ~2,000 years dead; colonists settle promptly. |
| Dragonborn exodus → Zealots retake Jotunheim | ~2,900 | NW-Musleheim tunnels cool enough to walk ~3,000; within a century the zealots go — still *before* Kuru's founding, so Kuru stays all-human. |
| Kuru founded (≈100% human) | ~2,800–2,500 | Founded *after* the dragonborn have gone — so the colonists are all human. |
| Kuru rebels → the Order of Sunne is born | ~2,500–2,200 | A few centuries of colonial chafe after the founding. |
| Kalikhat Crusades (Kuru's first) | ~2,000–1,600 | After Kuru's independence, and once Kalikhat's surface is habitable again (~2,000). |
| **The Bad Pass (Ragnarok II)** | **~1,000** | Comet pass 6; sabotaged → the Muspel empire collapses and its people mutate into orcs. |
| Mini Dark Age → orc raids → Kuru's reconquest → now | 1,000 → now | Paced out of the bad pass: ~300 yrs of ruin, then emergence, the Viking raids, and the turn. |
| The coming Ragnarok | ~now (~1 yr) | Comet pass 7 — one cycle after the last. |

*The throughline: pin the **Bleeding Star at ~5,000**, lay the **~1,000-year passes** over it, and
then **physics does the rest** — sick land stays empty for ages, new land can't be used before the
comet makes it, and the hot spot's slow drift decides when the tunnels open.*
