# The Floating Fleet — the Wandering Dragonborn (and Halfling) Sea-Civilization

The nomad half of dragonborn society: a whole civilization that lives on
the open water, never settling. [Blackacre](../characters/blackacre.md)'s
people. Pulled together from the 5/27–6/10/26 backstory chat. The other
dragonborn faction — the insular **Lonka zealots** — are their mirror;
both worship the same dragon (see
[taniyn-and-lonka.md](taniyn-and-lonka.md)).

> Legend: **[settled]** = grounded in chat · **[loose]** = floated, not
> locked · **[draft]** = invented here for the DM to keep or cut.
>
> ⚠️ **Player owns this lore.** The DMs explicitly handed **Ahms**
> narrative control of the sea-fleet's culture — *"whatever tall tales
> you tell the party about the sea fleets will instantly become canon."*
> Treat everything here as **his** to overrule. **[settled — per 5/28]**

## What they are

- A **separate version of civilization on a water world** — wandering
  fleets that go out into the open ocean for **months or years** at a
  time. The inspiration is the **ancient Phoenicians** of the
  Mediterranean: sea-traders and colonists, not a kingdom. **[settled]**
- **Majority halfling**, with a **fair share of nomadic dragonborn** and a
  scattering of **all the other races**. **[settled]**
- Organized as a **chiefdom — many tribal heads**, no single ruler. The
  fleet is **big**; no one captain has fleet-wide pull (which is why
  **Sayid** could only ever co-opt *a few* tribes, not the whole).
  **[settled]**

## Endonym & exonym

- Outsiders call them **barbarians** or **pirates** — and the campaign
  leans on the real etymology (*barbarian* ← the same root that gives us
  *Berber*). **[settled]**
- Internally, the dragonborn among them are the **Amatssou** —
  *"living beyond the fear,"* a name for the far-flung nature of their
  wandering. Blackacre is **Amatssou**, descended from forebears who left
  the towns flanking Lonka (e.g. **Ka**) generations ago for the fleet.
  **[settled]**

## Life on the water — and why a druid is gold

- They **maintain the fleet almost entirely from the sea**: seaweed, fish
  bone, hide, sinew, bladder. A floating world that resupplies itself.
  **[settled]**
- **The one thing the ocean cannot give them is timber** — masts, spars,
  hulls. So every few years the fleets come into **island-rich waters to
  harvest wood**, and those timber-runs are when the fleet touches the
  wider world. **[settled]**
- **Therefore druids are a precious commodity.** A druid can **grow and
  repair timber** — the single irreplaceable resource. This is the keystone
  of Blackacre's concept: not "sea druid = merman," but **a plant-druid
  whose magic is the most valuable thing on a ship of wood.** His parents
  were druids too (see his backstory). **[settled — the character's whole
  hook]**

## Boats

- **All variations**, from **sail-canoes** (Caribbean/outrigger style,
  built for rivers and open sea) and **sloops** up to larger barques.
  Small, fast, quiet craft **outrun and outmaneuver** the **galleys** that
  the land kingdoms (**Kuru, Noku Kurunatuka, Suartleheim**) field as their
  dominant power. **[settled]**
- Blackacre's own boat is a **commandeered sloop / sail-canoe** he took
  fleeing to Mukka. **[settled]**

## Music — the fleet's signature

Music is **central** to the culture, and it's also **practical**:

- **Arabic/Berber polyrhythms** are used to **beat commands across a fleet
  in naval combat** — drum-code over open water.
- **Big amplified war-drums** on the boats.
- **Whale-bladder stretched over entire hulls** so the **ship itself
  becomes a drum.** A fleet maneuvering is a wall of polyrhythmic thunder.
  **[settled — Ahms's invention; great set-piece audio]**

## The lost tribes (the Phoenician dragonborn)

A standing quest seam from 5/28: **dragonborn are the Phoenicians of Nila** —
the **trade-and-colony** people, not a war-state. Their mother-city is
**Lonka, their Tyre**: at once the holy first-perch of Taniyn (see
[taniyn-and-lonka.md](taniyn-and-lonka.md)) *and* the metropolis their
colonists scattered from across the seas. Somewhere out among those
colonies is a **ruined ancient dragonborn settlement** in the
Carthage/Tunisia mold — **the conquerors salted the earth and massacred
the inhabitants**, but **survivors cling on in hidden coves and cliffside
caves**, integrated with local peoples and half-forgotten. **Lost tribes
of dragonborn, scattered to the far corners** — a find-the-lost-tribes
quest that rhymes with both **Beowulf's** fruitless search
([maalm-founding.md](maalm-founding.md)) and the **children-of-Taniyn**
cosmology. **[loose — offered, not locked]**

> **Two Punic threads, deliberately split.** The *trade-and-colony*
> Phoenicians are **here** — the dragonborn, Lonka-as-Tyre. The *Punic War*
> (Hannibal, the crossings, a Zama) lives with the **orc Muspel war-state**
> instead, recast as Viking/barbarian herdsmen who fight their **own** wars
> (see [the Muspel empire §5](the-muspel-empire.md)). Carthage's mercantile,
> mercenary-hiring, Sicily-obsessed side carries over to neither.

## Showing up in play

- The DMs floated the **fleet arriving mid-adventure** — invading an
  island the party is on, or a **huge sea-battle offshore** while the PCs
  are ashore. Stage the whale-bladder drums for it. **[loose]**
- If it's **Sayid's** tribes, Blackacre **can't hide who he is** (see his
  character ref). If it's any other tribe, he might pass unrecognized.
  **[settled]**
