## 1. Seafaring — the three ages of the ship

In a world that is *mostly water* and broken into islands, **transport is the master
technology.** It has come in three layers, each still partly present:

- **Dragons (the first age).** The earliest crossings were made **on the backs of literal
  dragons** — the children of Taniyn carrying people island to island ([Taniyn & Lonka](taniyn-and-lonka.md),
  [overview §5](campaign-overview.md)). As the dragons dwindled (the hot spot moved on, the
  gods slept as islands), this was lost.
- **The lashed pontoon (the long age — still here).** After the dragons, the **wandering
  Dragonborn** crossed in **small twin-pontoon sailboats** — roughly **six to a boat**,
  built **without a single nail,** every joint **lashed** (sinew, cord, pegged wood). For
  most of history these were the *only* link between islands, and they are **still in
  use:** the traditional craft of the [Floating Fleet](floating-fleet.md), the boats
  **Sam's** people sail yet. Cheap, repairable on any beach, seaworthy in skilled hands.
- **The nailed hull (the bold, brittle age).** Going **bigger** meant **nails** — and the
  first nails were **bronze.** A bronze-nailed hull can be larger, carry cargo, cross open
  water with a real hold — but it is **only as reliable as the metal trade that feeds it**
  (§2). 🔒 **The wreck in the [Shipwrecked Sailor](amseh-dynasty.md) tale is one of these:**
  an early, ambitious bronze-nailed vessel that **did not make it** — bound for **Lonka,**
  wrecked on the serpent's island along the way.

## 2. Metals — the ages of the blade

- **Bronze (the present age).** Nila runs on **bronze:** copper alloyed with **tin.**
  Copper is common enough; **tin is rare and far-flung,** so bronze **only exists where
  long trade routes do.** This is the hidden fragility beneath every empire: *no tin road,
  no bronze.*
  - `[OPEN — come back to this]` **Source the copper and the tin** on Nila's map, and **add
    both to the trade-goods lists inscribed in the [Valley of the Kings tombs](../encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md)**
    (beside the guano, ivory, incense, apes). Working proposal: **copper** near the old
    Maalm mainland; **tin** from a *single distant island,* so the route is long and
    cuttable. *(Flagged to develop.)*
- **The Bronze-Age Collapse = the Bleeding Star.** `[settled — thematically]` As our own
  Bronze Age fell partly because the **component trade collapsed,** the [Bleeding Star
  (~5,000 ya)](amseh-dynasty.md) and its **Sea Peoples cut the trade webs** bronze depended
  on. When the tin stopped coming, the weapons stopped coming — part of why the Amseh world
  fell so completely and stayed down so long.
- **Iron from the fallen stars (the only "steel" so far).** `[settled]` Nila has **no iron
  industry yet.** The one iron-grade metal in use is **meteoric — forged from the fallen
  stars** (as Egypt's first iron was *bia-n-pet,* "iron of the sky"). It is **rare, sacred,
  and superior,** the metal of **kings and god-touched weapons** — and it ties straight into
  the comet cosmology: *the sky drops the stone that becomes gods, beasts, islands... and
  the rare blade.* (Kuru's **godstone** is the same fallen-star material at its extreme.)
- **The coming Iron Age.** `[loose / decision]` Nila is **poised at the edge of smelted
  iron**, on two real facts:
  - **Volcanic ground is iron-rich** (basalt, black magnetite sands). So **iron lives where
    the hot spot has built new land — Musleheim, the Kurunatukas, the young volcanic
    isles.** *(A sharp irony: the orc homeland and the contested new islands are the world's
    **iron,** not just its soil — another reason everyone wants them.)* `[OPEN]` pin where
    iron-working first takes hold; lead is the **volcanic north / Kurunatukas,** putting the
    Iron Age in the hands of whoever holds the new land.
  - **Steel is a stretch — for now.** Bloomery iron needs sustained **~1,100–1,300 °C**
    (charcoal + bellows → wrought iron to carburize); **melting / crucible steel wants
    ~1,500 °C+.** Nila can *reach* bloomery iron when a culture commits, but **dependable
    steel is not here yet** — which is exactly why **fallen-star metal** is still king of
    blades. `[loose]` volcanic/geothermal heat as a flavorful shortcut for whoever cracks
    it first.

## 3. Why it matters at the table

- **Trade is a cuttable weak point.** Bronze hangs on the tin road; the present war hangs on
  **borrowed [Mukka](kuru-and-the-phoenix.md) ships.** Sam's fleet cutting supply lines — or
  a tin route failing — topples a power **without a battle.** The Bronze-Age-Collapse lever,
  reusable.
- **Star-metal is a quest reward with cosmic weight.** A meteoric blade is not just *+1*;
  it's *a piece of the sky,* kin to the gods and the godstone — on-theme for names &
  ownership and the comet cycle.
- **Whoever reaches iron first changes the world** — and the iron is on the **volcanic,
  contested** islands. The next age is a prize on the board.
