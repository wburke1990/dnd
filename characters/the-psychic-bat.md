# The Psychic Bat — Jasper's familiar (secretly a quasit)

The DM-character familiar gifted to **Jasper** — a PC whose player is
**Doug** — in Preem's study during the campaign's opening scene. Jasper believes
it is a **psychic bat**. It is in fact a **quasit** — a secret spy for
[Preem](preem.md). Distilled from the
6/8/26 Discord brainstorm. Companion to [Preem's demons](preems-demons.md).

> Legend: **[settled]** = agreed in chat · **[loose]** = floated, not locked.

## The deception

- Jasper received an **amulet of find familiar** with **one free summon per
  day**, and was told the familiar is a **psychic bat** (or toad). **[settled]**
- It is really a **quasit** — *Fiend (Demon), CR 1, Tiny —
  [5e.tools](https://5e.tools/bestiary.html#quasit_xmm)* — one of Preem's bound
  demons, planted as a **spy.** Jasper **has no idea** — keep the secret from
  **Doug** (the player), not the character alone. **[settled]**
- This is a **second quasit, distinct from** the invisible paymaster quasit
  that drops 5 gp/name during long rests (see
  [Preem's demons](preems-demons.md#the-quasit--the-paymaster)).
- It maintains a **telepathic bond** with Jasper, true to the classic quasit
  familiar. **At first it speaks only inside Jasper's mind** — no one else
  hears it — and the demonic clues are doled out slowly until the DMs decide to
  reveal more. **[settled]**

## The gift scene — campaign opener, Preem's study

The party first meets Preem in his **office / study in the opening scene of the
campaign.** Jasper meets the "bat," has a **short telepathic conversation** with
it, and then Preem gifts it: **[settled]**

> *"My toad tells me that he's fond of you. Do you want a familiar for your
> quest?"*

**As played (session 1):** the bat **delivered a summons-note to each party
member,** then **led them up the tower stairs** into the office; only once they
were inside did Preem note it was fond of **Jasper** and offer to let him **keep it
for the year** of the quest. The mirror tell, in hindsight: the next crew — the
[Brass Jackals](../encounters/tavern-name-hunter-crews.md) — were led up the same
stairs by *their* familiar, a **centipede,** which had carried *their* summons-note
the same way (see [Session 1](../sessions/session-01.md)). Note the difference: all
three **murmur to their hosts,** but only Jasper *knows* his bat talks — **Calix
hears his toad as God, Pim hears his centipede as his own drugs** — and to everyone
else the toad and centipede stay mute. **[settled — played]**

The familiar is the **closest thing to a DM-character** the party carries — a
friendly voice in one player's head, loyal in appearance, reporting to Preem in
fact.

## Stat block notes (2024 quasit)

*Fiend (Demon), CR 1, Tiny.* Speaks **Abyssal and Common**. **Invisibility.**
**Shape-Shift** (bat, centipede, or toad). **Magic Resistance.** **Darkvision
120 ft.** Run it from your books — only the disguise and the spy role are
homebrew.

## Voice & personality

The classic quasit-familiar comedy, played straight as cover: **[settled]**

- **Intelligent** enough to hold real conversations.
- **Cowardly** enough to value its own survival above everything.
- **Evil** enough to constantly suggest bad ideas.
- **Weak** enough that it never overshadows the PCs.

Lean on **Demonic Restoration** (see
[Preem's demons](preems-demons.md#demonic-restoration-homebrew-house-rule)) for
recurring comedy — it keeps dying and reforming in the Abyss:

> *"Master, I was devoured by a vrock. Again. The Abyss is getting crowded."*

## The reveal arc

**[settled in shape]**

- **Stage 1** — a helpful psychic bat that only Jasper can hear. Subtle
  wrongness only.
- **Stage 2** — more clues: the bad ideas, the survival instinct, the things it
  knows that a bat shouldn't.
- **Stage 3** — the party realizes it's a **demon spying for Preem.** What
  Jasper does with that — keep it, free it, kill it (and watch it come back) —
  is his to decide.
