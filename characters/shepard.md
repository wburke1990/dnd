# Shepard (SH3P4RD) — Preem's Butler, the Warforged Who Forgot

> **NPC.** An **old-guard Kuru warforged** (one of the first hundred) who **died on
> the first crusade,** spent centuries as a **ship's prow** and then a **town
> statue,** and was **recently resurrected by [Preem](preem.md)** to serve as his
> **butler** — and has now been **sent out with one of Preem's many name-hunting groups (not the PCs').** He remembers none of it.
> Full warforged context: [Kuru & the phoenix](../lore/kuru-and-the-phoenix.md).
> Legend: `[settled]` · `[loose]` · `[OPEN]` · 🔒 DM secret.

---

## Who he was

- `[settled]` An **old-guard warforged, Warforged 34,** designation **SH3P4RD**
  ("Shepard") — one of the **first hundred,** from the era when warforged were still
  **named and had personality** (see [Numbers and names](../lore/kuru-and-the-phoenix.md)).
  Old guard **painted their name onto their number plate;** his may still be legible on
  whatever is left of him.
- 🔒 `[settled]` **He is far rarer than he looks.** Of the first hundred, **no more than
  ~15 (probably ~8) survive** — so a low-number original like **#34** is an enormous
  prize. **Even [Aniess](aniess.md),** who knows exactly what a lost brethren is worth,
  would be **stunned** to learn the butler's number.
- `[settled]` He **died — drowned, or fell — on the [first crusade to Kalikhat](../lore/kuru-and-the-phoenix.md)**
  (§5), a **comrade of [Vigil](../lore/kuru-and-the-phoenix.md)** (the mist-warforged)
  from the same doomed expedition. Where Vigil's other fallen brethren ended up
  **stockpiled in his tower basement,** Shepard's body **drifted away into a stranger
  afterlife** (below).

## The long afterlife of a body

- `[settled]` His body was **recovered, somehow,** after his death.
- `[settled]` He served as the **prow of a ship** for a while.
- `[settled]` Then he stood as a **statue in a mining town in western Suartleheim**
  (Suartleheim Weet — the Svartalfheim dwarf-and-dark-elf isle;
  [overview §5](../lore/campaign-overview.md)).
- 🔒 Through all of it, what made the object precious was the **finite phoenix-stone**
  in him — godstone that Kuru never reclaimed, hidden in plain sight as ship and statue.

## Resurrection by Preem

- `[settled]` **Recently [Preem](preem.md) resurrected him** — `[OPEN]` **mechanism
  unknown.**
- `[settled]` He now works as **Preem's butler / personal attendant.** Preem **runs
  many parties out hunting names** ([overview](../lore/campaign-overview.md)), and has
  **sent Shepard out with one of them — not the PCs' group.** *(He is the "lost
  brethren" [Aniess](aniess.md) means to reach: she is in a **different** group and is
  angling to **intercept his** — [party roster](party-roster.md).)*
- 🔒 `[OPEN]` It is **not clear whether he has any biological parts left, or a soul.**
- 🔒 `[loose]` It is always possible **Preem did something naughty** to remake him —
  e.g. **used someone else's body parts** (or worse) to rebuild him. A live thread for
  the name/soul themes ([house rules](../lore/house-rules-and-world-mechanics.md)).

## What he doesn't know — and the danger he's in

- `[settled]` He has **lost his memories** and has **no idea** what he is, was, or is
  worth. He believes himself, more or less, Preem's butler.
- 🔒 `[settled]` He is **walking finite godstone** ([the economy of the stone](../lore/kuru-and-the-phoenix.md)).
  **Any other warforged who recognizes him will try to kill him for his stone.** The
  road is lethal to him in a way he can't perceive.
- 🔒 `[settled]` **[Aniess](aniess.md) (Greg), in a different one of Preem's groups, is
  hunting him** — she means to **intercept his group and deliver him to Kuru for glory,**
  repatriating his stone, without him knowing ([party roster](party-roster.md)).
- **The thesis on legs.** A warforged who has lost his **name's worth of self — his
  memory, maybe his soul, maybe even his own body** — is the
  [Names & Ownership](../lore/campaign-overview.md) themes embodied in a single figure:
  his **name survives on his plate** while the **person behind it is gone,** and
  everyone who knows what he is values him as a **thing** (stone, glory, a butler)
  rather than a who.

## Vigil knows him — the big reveal 🔒

`[settled]` Shepard and **[Vigil](../lore/kuru-and-the-phoenix.md) were comrades** on
the first crusade, and the memory runs **one way: Vigil still remembers Shepard;
Shepard remembers nothing — not even Vigil.** If they ever meet it is a **huge
reveal:** a friend long dead comes walking into the fog, **resurrected and hollow,** in
front of the mad holdout who has spent a millennium **keeping his fallen brethren** in
his basement. `[OPEN]` what Vigil does with that — **grief, recognition, a flash of
lucidity, a lunge for the stone, or all of them at once** — and whether the encounter
is how **Shepard first learns who he was.**
