## Backstory

Aniess was an orphaned elf — or believes she was. She has fragmented memories of scrounging and thieving on the streets as a child. She does not remember much before the church took her in.

The Order of the Emplere Cnihtas found her as a street urchin and recruited her alongside others. She was trained as a warrior and assassin — part of a covert intelligence network within the Order known only to the Warforged leadership. The acolytes who fill out the Order's public ranks do not know this arm exists.

The Order performed a ritual on Aniess and others in her cohort, merging them with other beings to grant them enhanced capabilities. Aniess was merged with a snake. She became Yuan-ti — half elf, half serpent — gaining strong resistance to magic and other features. She does not remember much of the ritual itself.

This is the Order's version of the Witcher's Trial — the transformation of recruits into something more useful than human. Aniess accepted it because she had no frame of reference for refusing. The Order was all she knew.

This is her first mission outside the compound.

## DM Note — The Larger Picture

Aniess does not know who she is at the most fundamental level. She thinks she's an orphaned elf. She isn't — or at least, that story is incomplete. She was taken, transformed into something useful, trained to be a weapon, and sent into the world believing she's serving a righteous cause.

The dead man's story is essentially a story about someone who used power over another being and called it something else. The Order did exactly this to Aniess. She will not make that connection consciously during the dream quest, but the player should feel it.

The demon wife is also a mirror for Aniess specifically. The demon was taken, transformed into something useful, bound in service to someone else's needs, and her inner life was never consulted. Aniess's inner life was shaped by the Order from childhood. Neither of them chose what they became.

**Her confession in the Hall of Two Truths:**

Aniess presents unique difficulty for the confession mechanic. She has been trained to give nothing of herself. She may not have conventional sins in the way other characters do — she was following orders she believed were righteous.

But that is the confession. Her heart is full of someone else's convictions.

*"I don't know if anything I believe is true. I don't know if I chose any of this. I don't know who I was before they took me. I have never asked."*

That last line echoes the dead man's confession deliberately. He never asked her. Aniess never asked herself.

Anubis receives her confession differently from the others — not with more or less weight, but with something that might be recognition. She is not being judged for what was done to her. But she is being asked to see it clearly.

**Her Yuan-ti nature and the demon wife:**

Once the demon wife's type is decided, consider whether her true form has serpentine qualities. If so, Aniess — Yuan-ti, half serpent — shares something with the demon wife without either of them knowing it. That parallel may surface eventually.

**The Order and the wizard:**

The Order uses transformed beings as weapons and intelligence assets. The wizard uses bound demons as slaves. These are structurally identical operations. Aniess is inside one of them without knowing it. When the party discovers the wizard's demon army, Aniess will be standing there having just escorted a soul through an afterlife built around the horror of binding.

Someone may point out the parallel. Or no one will, and the player will sit with it alone.
