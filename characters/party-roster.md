## Sam — Blackacre (Adghal ibn Omar ibn Omar Amtssou)
**Druid · Dragonborn · Individual · Profession: Sailor**

**Background.** Avoiding responsibility for his role in a fleet revolt. He and a
good friend orchestrated a seizure of a few ships of a fleet, but he lost his
nerve / faith in the plan when it got violent, and is trying to avoid that fleet
and that history. Maybe the friend is now a big player; maybe the friend lost his
life in the process after Blackacre left — whatever is interesting. Grew up with
the floating fleet, but his "port" of call is **Tripoca**.

- **Idea:** tie into **Mukka**.
- **History (the floating fleet):** wandering fleets go out for months or years
  at a time into the open oceans, doing maintenance with seaweed and fish bones,
  etc. — basically a separate version of civilization on our water world. Maybe he
  joined one. All races are aboard, but the majority are halflings. The only thing
  they can't get out of the ocean is **masts for ships**, so they come into the
  island-rich areas of the world every few years to harvest timber. Sam gets to
  create whatever sea-life stories he wants, and we'd give him control of that part
  of the lore.
- **Mechanic:** **whatever tall tales Sam tells the party about the sea fleets
  instantly become canon.**
- **Key detail:** as a **Druid he can repair and grow timber** — HUGE on a ship.
- **Inspiration (from Sam):** *The Dream Archipelago* (Christopher Priest) — a
  travelogue-style archipelago world of unreliable narrators, characters who go by
  **different names on different islands**, and **islands that move**. Strong
  resonance with Blackacre's many names (Adghal / Blackacre), the
  tall-tales-become-canon mechanic, and Nila's islands that come and go.

## Greg — Aniess
**Rogue / Cleric · Yuan-Ti / Elf · Individual · Profession: Spy for the Warforged Crusaders**

**Background.** Street-urchin elf (in **Suartleheim**) recruited by the Warforged
crusaders to be part of a sect of spy/assassins they use across Nila. Each spy is
transformed through a magical ritual into a new race — sold to the individual as
giving them specific attributes and helping them blend in / proving they'll
sacrifice all for the Crusaders; for the Crusaders, it's to transform them into
**lesser races** and deepen their subservience. *Inspiration: Witchers.*

**Quest motivation.** Gathering intel in the city; attends many of these "call for
adventurers" as a ruse to stay current on the comings and goings. Accepts the quest
when she sees the Warforged and realizes it's a lost brethren. It's a personal
attendant of the Wizard, so she knows she cannot attempt anything there. She could
inform her Masters — or better, carefully direct the Warforged back to **Kuru** and
be enshrined in glory for returning one of the brethren. She is not directly
assigned to the group with the Warforged, but abandoning this group would make it
even harder to track and ideally intercept the other group to mastermind her plan.
For now she bides her time, and when the stars align she delivers the Warforged to
Kuru with none the wiser.

**Mechanics for Greg — make the transformation really meaningful:**
- The stone that creates an island is a **snake**.
- **Snake Island** has Yuan-Ti on it. There is a **Warforged** on this island (it
  predates the mind-control, or is too far to be reached); it's been on the island
  for thousands of years.
- The Yuan-Ti **cannot leave** because of the experiment. People on the island are
  **born with snakelike characteristics.**
- [The Warforged] shared the spell with the Warforged [Crusaders], and they use it
  to transform [recruits].
- On the island they use the transformation as a **defense mechanism** to blend in
  with the local snakes (island of huge snakes) — because the **island spirit is a
  giant snake.**

**Warforged Crusaders:** an ancient intellectual sect that was dispersed for
**research on the magical stones** — and basically they're living in an alternate
reality.

## Jeremy — Sarric Ashgazer
**Ranger · Firbolg · Group · Ecoterrorism: "Restoring our Clean Kingdom"**

**Background.** Most firbolgs are kind and caring. Sarric isn't mean, but
**ill-tempered**. He grew up with firbolgs who also cared for nature, but when
empires came in to destroy the environment to build and expand, they did nothing.
Sarric couldn't do anything to stop that as a kid — but now, as an adult, he can.
The hive he carries is the one he saved when his home was destroyed, and he has
been caring for it since that day.

- *Inspiration: Planeteers (but radical).*
- **Quest motivation:** make some money, but also gain any insight into what the
  empire is up to at large if they are attempting to destroy more nature.
- **Theme:** transformation and identity.
