## Backstory

Sarric grew up in a small Firbolg village, learning from his community's passive and quiet nature. His childhood was typical and happy until a soldier arrived with news that the local lord had purchased their land and intended to destroy it for profit. The villagers refused to leave and requested peaceful negotiations. The next day soldiers arrived and began cutting trees and razing the land.

Sarric looked to the adults to fight back. They didn't. They picked up what they could and fled, unwilling to take lives. Something broke in Sarric in that moment — the knowledge that his home could simply be taken and that the people he trusted most would not stop it.

While fleeing with his family he grabbed a lone beehive. It was the one thing he could save.

As he grew older he formed a conviction: the rich and powerful do not care about the poor and kind, and nobody is coming to stop them. He practiced fighting. He learned the bow. He left home with a mission — to protect the weak from the powerful, to defend nature from those who would destroy it for profit.

For years he wandered, intervening wherever he found the powerful hurting the weak. The faces of those he helped confirmed for him that his mission was just.

But he went too far. The worst moment: he found a camp of men hired to remove people from their homes. A few fire arrows. A blazing inferno. The screams he heard were not only the screams of men.

He did not look back. He fled into the woods.

In a nearby town, wanted posters appeared — a cloaked figure with bees buzzing around him. He ran again.

He later met Jasper and Pax, who shared enough of his beliefs to work alongside. They formed the organization together.

## Personality and Beliefs

- Trusts very few people. Those who love nature and want to preserve it are the exception — he trusts them inherently.
- Deep disdain for the extremely wealthy who believe money gives them the right to harm others.
- Not religious, but believes the world feels pain when it is hurt. As a Firbolg he can speak imperfectly with plants and animals — not with spell-level clarity, but enough to sense something.
- Conflicted about his family. They are alive and they love him. He has never fully forgiven them for not fighting back.
- Headstrong. Capable of blinding himself with his own convictions.

## Notable Details

**The beehive:** Sarric has carried a beehive since the day his village was destroyed. He has an infinite bag of bees. This is both a practical resource and a piece of his identity — the one thing he saved from his home.

**Wanted:** He is actively wanted for the camp fire. The poster describes a cloaked figure with bees. In cities he needs to be careful. The wanted status is unresolved and may become relevant.

**The camp:** He set fire to a camp of men hired to displace people from their homes. The fire killed people who were not soldiers — he heard screaming that told him so. He did not go back. He has never spoken of it.

## DM Note — The Camp

The camp is Sarric's central wound. He committed an act of violence that killed people beyond his intended targets, and he ran from it. He has continued doing good work since, and the good work is real — but it is also partly a way of not thinking about the screams.

His confession in the Hall of Two Truths will almost certainly be about the camp. He will want to contextualize it — the lord, the village, the injustice that came before. Let him. Then let the silence do the rest. The scales don't weigh context.

The confession in its simplest form: *"I set a fire and I heard screaming and I didn't go back."*

Whether he arrives at that simplicity on his own is up to Jeremy.
