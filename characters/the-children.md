# The Children of [PLACEHOLDER — DEAD MAN'S NAME]

## Role in the Campaign

The children are the party's primary contact for the dream quest. They hire the party to shepherd their father's soul through the afterlife in exchange for lifting the curse and providing access to the family's resources. They are recurring NPCs who may appear in future sessions as allies, employers, or complications.

## Personality

**The older child (son) — played by David:**
He has made a kind of peace with his parents' history. He has decided that the love his mother showed them was real and that is enough. He is practical, forward-thinking, and carries grief the way people do when they have had a long time to prepare for something. After the dream quest he feels lighter — not happy, but done, in the way of someone who has waited a long time for something to be over and finally it is. He helps the party up without being asked when they wake. He is already thinking about what comes next.

**The younger child (daughter) — played by you:**
She is less resolved. She keeps looking at the door her mother used to stand in. She is not sure it is right to feel relieved. When the party wakes from the dream quest she starts to ask — *"Did he — was it —"* — and then stops. She realizes she doesn't want the answer. Or that there isn't one to give. She will take longer to find her footing after this night than her brother will.

## What They Know

- Their father freed their mother from a cruel wizard using her true name and Planar Binding
- He renewed the binding every year for thirty-one years
- They were raised to understand this as protection — he cast it to make sure she couldn't be taken back
- Their mother loved them without reservation. They are certain of this.
- Their mother spoke one sentence after their father died, to no one in particular: *"I don't know what I would have chosen."* They heard it. They have been living with it since.
- Their mother stayed for a time after his death — they disagree on how long — and then was gone one morning. They did not see her leave.

## What They Don't Know

- What their mother would have chosen, had she been free
- What happened to their father in the Hall of Two Truths
- The full extent of their demonic heritage and what it means for their futures

## DM NOTE — PRIVATE

**Why the children cannot do the dream quest themselves:**

Their souls are half-anchored to the Abyss in a way that makes the Duat inaccessible to them. They cannot enter the river of stars because they are not fully of the mortal world that feeds into it. They are not fully of the demon world either. They exist in between, and the door to the afterlife does not open for them.

If the party asks why they can't do it themselves, the children say:

*"We have tried. The dream does not take us. We fall asleep and we simply — sleep. We don't know if it is because of what we are, or because we loved him, or because the door knows we are not ready to stand before that judgment ourselves. We only know that you can go where we cannot."*

This answer is honest and slightly ambiguous. The children do not fully understand their own nature. They were raised on a particular story about their parents and they believe it. Do not over-explain.

**The truth about their mother:**

Their mother was bound to the material plane by their father using her true name, renewed every year. She does not know what she would have chosen, because the binding made the question permanently unanswerable even to her. The sentence she spoke — *"I don't know what I would have chosen"* — is the most honest thing anyone in this story ever said.

**Their demonic heritage as a future hook:**

The children are powerful spellcasters. As they grow into their power, subtle aspects of their demonic heritage may begin to manifest — the nature of which depends on their mother's demon type once that is decided. This is a long-term campaign hook, not an immediate concern.

## The Bargain

In exchange for the party shepherding their father's soul:

- The curse is lifted (if applicable)
- Healing potions at cost
- Spell components at cost
- A safe house in the city
- Their personal assistance, once each, if the party calls for it

They honor this bargain fully and without resentment. They are grateful. They just don't know how to show it very well on the night it happens.
