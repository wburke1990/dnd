# Bram Hale — rival NPC (the Lapis Writ)

Human. The **Lapis Writ's** mirror for **[Jasper](jasper.md)** — *the man you
become if you join the strong instead of fighting them.* Full backstory and the
mirror's design live in the tavern scene:
**[tavern-name-hunter-crews.md → Group 1, the Lapis Writ](../encounters/tavern-name-hunter-crews.md)**.

> **In one line:** the same profit-driven disaster that forged Jasper wrecked
> Bram's home — but where Jasper fights *for* the weak, Bram decided never to be
> weak again and sold his strength and loyalty to the powerful. Warm, open, no
> guilt. The party will *like* him, and liking him is the trap.

## Voice — sample lines (to actually charm Jasper)

Bram's two hooks into Jasper are **a shared wound** (oligarchs wrecked both their
homes) and **total honesty** (Jasper respects people who hide nothing). None of
these are lies — that's what makes them land.

**1. Recognition — open with kinship, not a pitch.**

> *"You've got the look — the one where you count the exits even in a friendly
> room. I wore it for years. Who'd you lose?"*
>
> *"Earth genasi. Stone in the blood. They dig the ground out from under people
> like us, then act surprised when one of us comes back made of it."*

**2. The reasonable poison — warm, never preachy.**

> *"Only thing I learned that day, and it's free: the world eats the weak. Every
> time. You can spend your life angry at the teeth — or stop being the meal. I
> stopped."*
>
> *"You fight so it won't happen to the next town. I admire that, truly. But it
> happened to the next town. And the one after. The strong don't lose, friend —
> they just change whose name's on the deed."*

**3. The closer — leave the hook, twist gently.**

> *"If the cause ever stops loving you back — and it will, they always do — come
> find me. There's always work for a man who's survived something."*
>
> *"You and me started in the same hole. I'm just the one who climbed out by
> stepping on people. Sleep on which of us is happier."*

## How to play him

Never defensive, never a recruiter. If Jasper attacks the cause — or Bram
himself — Bram *agrees* (*"You're not wrong"*) and stays warm. The horror is that
he's kind, honest, funny, and damned, and Jasper leaves the table having
*enjoyed* him.
