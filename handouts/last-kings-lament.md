# The Last King's Lament — Player Handout

*The poem given to players in the Tomb 4 inner chamber (Amseh IV, the last king's unfinished tomb), set above the little coffins. A Mesopotamian-style lament. Verbatim, canonical (matches [encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md](../encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md)). This is the edited-down version; the longer draft used to generate the image is recorded in [prompts/tomb-4-inner-chamber-lament.md](../prompts/tomb-4-inner-chamber-lament.md).*

---

Oh gods of my father, why have you forsaken me? Your voices which spoke so loud are silent. The day shines bright over the Land, but for me the day is black. The bright day has become a dark day. Tears, lament, anguish and despair are lodged within me. I shall be the last king of this land. I offer you my children as penance, yet still you are silent. I have no heirs. The people starve and the land is dry and barren.
