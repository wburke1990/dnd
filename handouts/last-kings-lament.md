# The Last King's Lament — Player Handout

*The poem given to players in the Tomb 4 inner chamber (Amseh IV, the last king's unfinished tomb), set above the little coffins. A Mesopotamian-style lament. Verbatim. A shorter variant is quoted in [encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md](../encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md); source request and image prompt: [prompts/tomb-4-inner-chamber-lament.md](../prompts/tomb-4-inner-chamber-lament.md).*

---

oh gods of my father, why have you forsaken me? Your voices which spoke so loud are silent. the day shines bright over the Land, but for me the day is black. The bright day has become a dark day. Tears, lament, anguish and despair are lodged within me. Suffering overwhelms me like a weeping child. Let my mother who bore me not cease lamenting for me before you. Let my sister, truly a sweet-voiced singer, narrate tearfully to you the deeds by which I was overpowered. Let my wife voice my suffering to you. I shall be the last king of this land. I offer you my children as penance, yet still you are silent. I have no heirs. The people starve and the land is dry and barren
