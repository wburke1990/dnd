# The Tale of the Shipwrecked Sailor — Player Handout

*The text given to players in the Tomb 2 inner chamber. Verbatim. (Source request and image prompt: [prompts/tomb-2-inner-chamber-shipwrecked-sailor.md](../prompts/tomb-2-inner-chamber-shipwrecked-sailor.md).)*

---

I was voyaging to the mines of Pharaoh aboard a great ship with 150 sailors, the best of Egypt. But a storm arose, the waves grew eight cubits high, and the ship was destroyed. I alone survived, clinging to a piece of wood. A wave cast me upon an island. For three days I was alone. There I found figs, grapes, grain, fish, birds, and all good things. I made an offering unto the gods. Then I heard a noise like thunder. The earth shook, and a great serpent approached. He was thirty cubits long, his body overlaid with gold, and his color like true lazuli. He opened his mouth and said: 'What has brought thee, what has brought thee, little one, what has brought thee? If thou sayest not speedily what has brought thee to this isle, I will make thee know thyself; as a flame thou shalt vanish, if thou tellest me not something I have not heard, or which I knew not, before thee? 'What has brought thee, what has brought thee, little one, what has brought thee to this isle which is in the sea, and of which the shores are in the midst of the waves?' I told him of the shipwreck and how the sea had cast me upon the island. Then said he to me: "Fear not, fear not, little one, and make not thy face sad. If thou hast come to me, it is God who has let thee live. For it is He who has brought thee to this isle of the blest, where nothing is lacking, and which is filled with all good things. See now, thou shalt pass one month after another, until thou shalt be four months in this isle. Then a ship shall come from thy land with sailors, and thou shalt leave with them and go to thy country, and thou shalt die in thy town. "Converse is pleasing, and he who tastes of it passes over his misery, I will therefore tell thee of that which is in this isle. I was on it with my brothers, and children were in the midst of them. We totaled seventy-five snakes, my children together with my brothers; I will not mention to you a little daughter whom I had obtained by prayer. Then a star fell, and these went up in flame because of it. It happened that I was not with them when they burned. I was not among them. I was dead to them and would have died for them when I found them a heap of corpses all together.” "As for thee if thou art strong, and if thy heart waits patiently, thou shalt press thy infants to thy bosom and embrace thy wife. Thou shalt return to thy house which is full of all good things, thou shalt see thy land, where thou shalt dwell in the midst of thy kindred." I promised to tell Pharaoh of his greatness and to bring him treasures and offerings from Egypt. Then he smiled and said: "Thou art not rich in perfumes, for all that thou hast is but common incense. As for me I am prince of the land of Punt, and I have perfumes. Only the oil which thou sayedst thou wouldest bring is not common in this isle. But, when thou shalt depart from this place, thou shalt never more see this isle; it shall be changed into waves." When the ship arrived as he had foretold, I came before him once more. Then he said to me: 'Farewell, farewell, go to thy house, little one, see again thy children, and let thy name be good in thy town; these are my wishes for thee.' The serpent gave me gifts of perfumes, incense, ivory, apes, baboons, and many precious things. Then he said to me: 'Behold thou shalt come to thy country in two months, thou shalt press to thy bosom thy children, and thou shalt rest in thy tomb." I departed from the island, returned safely to Egypt, and brought the gifts before Pharaoh, my father.
