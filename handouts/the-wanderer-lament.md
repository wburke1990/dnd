# The Wanderer — Lament of Vanished Glory (Player Handout)

*The "Where is the horse, where the rider" passage from the Old English "The Wanderer" — used **twice** in play:*

*1. **As Musleheim lore** — an orc/Norse elegy for the broken war-host and the silent northern colonies, in the register of the fallen longship sea-power (see [the Muspel empire](../lore/the-muspel-empire.md)).*

*2. **Recited by Annibel in her cups** at the [tavern reveal](../lore/the-muspel-empire.md) — the half-orc general **Annibel/Hannibal** (David's Hannibal, she/her) after her Zama at Ilka Mak. She besieged Anklia and never took it; her cavalry and captains are gone; she drifts from court to court trading tactics for coin, unable to say who she was. "Where is the horse gone? Where the rider?" sits on a broken cavalry commander directly.*

*Real-world source: Old English "The Wanderer" (Exeter Book, c. 8th–10th c.), the lament of vanished glory (~ll. 92–96) — the passage J.R.R. Tolkien adapted for the Lament of the Rohirrim. Translations vary; this is the wording we chose. Verbatim; line breaks preserved.*

---

```
Where is the horse gone? Where the rider?
Where the giver of treasure?
Where are the seats at the feast? Where are the revels in the hall?
Alas the bright cup! Alas the mailed warrior!
Alas the prince's splendour! How that time has passed away,
dark under the cover of night, as if it had never been.
```
