Title: The Founding of Maalm — Tavern Mural

Create a single continuous horizontal mural in the style of an ancient Egyptian frieze, medieval tapestry, or painted tavern wall mural. The mural should read left-to-right as one uninterrupted narrative and feel ancient, legendary, and worn with age.

Color palette: ochre, sandstone, faded gold, deep red, charcoal black, weathered bronze.

The same group of characters should appear repeatedly as the story progresses.

LEFT PANEL — THE ARRIVAL

Show exactly thirteen figures arriving by boat on dark water.

One figure is a tall Dragonborn warrior with scales, a reptilian face, and a heroic bearing. The other twelve are a mixture of elves and men.

The Dragonborn should be visually prominent but still part of the group.

Ahead of them rises the half-buried skyline of a ruined Egyptian-inspired empire. Broken obelisks, collapsed temples, and sand-covered monuments should dominate the background.

MIDDLE PANEL — THE CONFRONTATION

Show the same thirteen figures standing before the ruins.

A vast staircase descends into darkness beneath the ancient city.

From this darkness emerges Ammit.

Ammit must be enormous compared to the figures.

She should have:

- the head and jaws of a crocodile
- the forelimbs, mane, and chest of a lion
- the hindquarters of a hippopotamus

Her jaws are open as she rises from the deep beneath the city.

The twelve companions stand together farther back.

The Dragonborn stands apart from them, closer to Ammit than anyone else, as if stepping forward to face her alone.

The composition should visually imply that the Dragonborn will descend to meet her.

The transition from this scene into the next should be gradual and seamless.

RIGHT PANEL — THE FOUNDING OF MAALM

Show exactly twelve figures.

The Dragonborn is absent.

The remaining elves and men are placing stones and raising the first foundations of a city.

Construction should feel hopeful but solemn.

The viewer should recognize these as the same companions from the earlier scenes.

The city is only beginning to rise. A few walls, scaffolds, unfinished towers, and foundation stones are visible.

The transition from the confrontation scene should suggest that the city is being built directly above the place where Ammit emerged.

GENERAL COMPOSITION

Continuous mural with no hard panel borders.
Ancient Egyptian storytelling style with repeated figures.
Worn plaster, cracks, faded pigments, and age damage.
Strong sense of myth and legend.
The Dragonborn appears in the first two scenes and is absent from the final scene.
Figure counts must be exact: 13 figures in the arrival scene, 13 figures in the confrontation scene, and 12 figures in the founding scene.
The mural should evoke sacrifice, loss, friendship, and the founding of a civilization.
