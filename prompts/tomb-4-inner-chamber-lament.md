The canonical player-facing lament was later edited down to the shorter version; for the text given to players see [handouts/last-kings-lament.md](../handouts/last-kings-lament.md) and [encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md](../encounters/valley-of-the-kings.md). tomb4 inner chamber reveal (lament)

i am making some art for my dnd campaign. I am doing a riff on the valley of the kings. one of the tombs contains the a Mesopotamian lament about the voices of the gods falling silent. I would like to make a wall of hieroglyphics and images to capture the feeling of the tale for my group. Here is the text I plan to give them: “oh gods of my father, why have you forsaken me? Your voices which spoke so loud are silent. the day shines bright over the Land, but for me the day is black. The bright day has become a dark day. Tears, lament, anguish and despair are lodged within me. Suffering overwhelms me like a weeping child. Let my mother who bore me not cease lamenting for me before you. Let my sister, truly a sweet-voiced singer, narrate tearfully to you the deeds by which I was overpowered. Let my wife voice my suffering to you. I shall be the last king of this land. I offer you my children as penance, yet still you are silent. I have no heirs. The people starve and the land is dry and barren” Below that poem, I will have little coffins in the unfinished tomb. could you make an image for me?
