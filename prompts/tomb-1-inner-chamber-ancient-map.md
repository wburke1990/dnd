### Master Prompt: Ancient Egyptian Pre-Cataclysm World Map

Create an ancient Egyptian tomb wall map depicting a world from approximately 6,000 years before the present age. The map should appear as a sacred cartographic mural painted on limestone and preserved inside a pharaoh's tomb.

**Style**

- Ancient Egyptian artistic conventions rather than modern cartography.
- Hand-painted on weathered papyrus or tomb plaster.
- Rich but faded mineral pigments: lapis blues, malachite greens, ochres, reds, and black ink outlines.
- Decorative hieroglyphic border around the map.
- Winged sun disk and Egyptian motifs incorporated into the frame.
- Symbolic rather than geographically precise.
- Minimal text; information conveyed through icons, symbols, and illustrations.

**Geography**

- Use the attached modern map as the base geography.
- Depict an earlier age before major meteor impacts and volcanic upheaval.
- Sea level is lower than the modern era.
- The large central island and the two smaller southeastern islands do not yet exist; replace them completely with open ocean.
- Coastlines should appear subtly different due to lower sea levels.
- Preserve the general arrangement of the surviving continents and islands.

**Northwestern Island**

- The large island in the northwest exists and should be fully illustrated.
- Moderately mountainous interior.
- Scattered forests and lakes.
- Smaller population than the modern era.
- Limited farmland.
- A few villages and perhaps one modest town.

**Population Density**

- This is a less populated ancient world.
- Reduce farmland substantially compared to the modern map.
- Cities should be smaller.
- Remove many secondary settlements.
- Villages should be sparse and isolated.
- Large stretches of wilderness should dominate the map.

**Map Symbols**

- Pink rectangles represent cities.
- Purple squares represent towns.
- Three black dots represent villages.
- Green stippling indicates forests.
- Green grass marks grasslands.
- Orange grid patterns indicate farmland.
- Grey mountains indicate mountain ranges.
- Blue shapes indicate lakes.
- Thick black coastlines indicate cliffs.

**Snake Island**

- At the extreme northeast corner of the map, place a tiny isolated island.
- The island should be almost insignificant in size.
- A visible giant serpent coils upon or around it.
- The serpent should evoke the snake from the Egyptian Tale of the Shipwrecked Sailor.
- Gold scales with lapis-blue accents.
- Presented as a mythic "here be monsters" warning.
- The serpent should be visible without enlarging the island.

**River**

- Add a short river flowing northward from the southern edge of the map.
- The river should connect to the pink city located on the peninsula near the bottom center-right.
- The river should extend only a short distance inland (roughly 10% of the map height).
- Do not place large rivers through the central ocean.

**Atmosphere**

- The map should feel ancient, sacred, and prophetic.
- A lost world before catastrophe.
- Civilizations are small compared to the vast wilderness.
- Emphasize mystery, forgotten kingdoms, divine geography, and mythological significance.

**Composition**

- Wide landscape orientation matching the original campaign map.
- Large central ocean.
- Clean readability for tabletop use.
- Suitable as a mural discovered in an ancient Egyptian tomb.
